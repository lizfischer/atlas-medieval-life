March  27.—Letter   to the Priory of Leominster, following  a formal 
                           visitation, forbidding it to lock the Church doors and prevent 
                           access for parochial uses, as had been done to the discouragement 
                           of reverence and devotion, and the danger of those who came for 
                           sanctuary  within.   The  Church  must  not be used for secular 
                           purposes on holy days.  The  use of the bells must not be stopped 
                           nor almsgiving curtailed.
