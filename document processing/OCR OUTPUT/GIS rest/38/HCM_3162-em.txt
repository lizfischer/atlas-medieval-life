HCM 3162-em
1306, 3 Ides May [May 13].
NOTARIAL INSTRUMENT drawn up and attested by John called Bouhs of London, notary public, quoting a papal bull of Pope Clement V addressed to W., bishop of Coventry and Lichfield, by which all grants and  concessions which Edward I had been compelled to make to certain of his nobles whilst he was campaigning in Flanders, were declared null and void. The paper bull is dated at Lyons on the kalends of January, 1305/06. The said W., bishop of Coventry and Lichfield, commands the dean and chapter of Hereford to promulgate this papal bull and make it known in the diocese of Hereford. Witnesses: John de Colonia, William Bouhs, clerks. Latin. Torn in the folds.
