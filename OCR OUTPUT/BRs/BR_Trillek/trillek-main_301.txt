Dec. 19.—On   the reference to him of a dispute between the Carmelite 
                         friars  and   John    de  Evesham,    rector  of  Ludlow,   the 
                          bishop  decides that  the bodies of  persons who   die im  the 
                         parish  of  Ludlow   but  are buried  elsewhere ought first, by 
                          the town’s custom, to be brought to the church, and  the offer- 
                          tngs should  belong to  the rector.  He   further directs that 
                          the Carmelites shall place  a bier in the church  as  if for a 
                         funeral  and  on  the Sunday   after the Epiphany   cause mass 
                          to be celebrated for the  soul of Sir Lawrence   de Lodelowe, 
                          who  was  buried  im  their  sanctuary  without  mass  in  the 
                         parish  church,  and  shall permit  the rector to  take all the 

                         offerings.
