HCM 1077
1309, November 2.
NOTARIAL INSTRUMENT drawn up and attested by Robert, called le Dorturer de Selebourne in the diocese of Winchester, notary public, recording that John de la Barewe, clerk, appeared before William de Tests, archidiaconus Aranensis in ecclesis Connen', and collector of the arrears of the six-yearly tenth levied by Pope Nicholas IV. John de la Barewe, clerk, was the proctor of the chapter of Hereford, and held letters of attorney from the chapter to prove his office, which letters are quoted in this instrument and dated the Saturday next before the Feast of the Apostles Simon and Judas in the year 1309. John de le Barewe presented an account of the six-yearly tenth collected by the dean and chapter of Hereford. Then follows an account of the details of the collection. Finally the dean and chapter were released from the rendering of the accounts by the archdeacon, who also affixed his seal to the instrument. Latin.
