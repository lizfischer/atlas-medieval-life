HCM 547
Late 13th. century
1. Nicholas de la Calewe de Chirchebreynton; 2. Dominus William de Foston', chaplain.
 GRANT, in consideration of two marks, of two acres of arable land at Chirchebreynton in the field called Chirchecrofth and lying together between the land belonging to the altar of the Blessed Mary of the church of Hereford on both sides and extending towards le Goldhord and containing six selions.
 Witnesses: John Lythfot, bailiff of the chapter, Bartholomew the apothecary, Richard de Bramford, John le Sypward, Phillip Howell', Roger franceys, John de Foston.
 Latin. Seal.550.
