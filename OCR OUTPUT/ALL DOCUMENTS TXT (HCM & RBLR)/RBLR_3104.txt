208 21 June 1331 Hartlebury Memorandum that the bishop collated the archdeaconry of Gloucester to Ds. Roger de Breynton, formerly rector of Hanbury-by-Droitwich (Hembur' iuxta Wich), it being vacant owing to the resignation of M. John de Usk because of the exchange of their benefices. He wrote to the prior of Worcester for his induction and the assignment of a stall in choir and place in chapter.(59) [See below]. (59) For this entry and the next one cf. no. 827, where they are combined, not being separately paragraphed in the MS. The dean of Gloucester was to see that Breynton received the appurtenances in the town. See no 355.
