The said Roger de Breynton', executor of the will of Richard de Swynesfeld', formerly bishop of Hereford, stated that the said Thomas Trillek and Walter Carles together with John, bishop of Hereford, Roger de Breynton’ himself, John de Usk*, and Nicholas de Lacrwent, coexecutors with them of the will of the late bishop of Winchester, owed to him a sum of five hundred pounds which bons unjustly withheld.
 Roger de Ereynton' further maintained that the said bishop, in the name of Adem, then bishop of Hereford, had bound himself in writing at “are on the twenty-seventh day of Mareh in the year 1310 in the sum of one hundred pounds to Roger himself, and to Richard de Mamenasche, Robert de Ikelesham, snd John de la Ffelde, canons of Hereford. ‘the said bishop Adam on the first day of February in the year 1319/20 at Shenyngfeld' had again bound himself in writing to the seme persons in the sum cf four hundred pounds, to be paid at specified terms. The said oe Adam had failed to discharge these obligations during his lifetimes as a result of the delay, the said Roger de Breynton’ had incurred a loss of a thousand pound se He produced the two documents to which he had referred. The executors through Nicholas de Rook’ confessed that oy could not cata the written bonds, and agreed that the said sage de ee should recover from them the debt and losses. he ssid Roger de Breynton' however acquitted them of the losses he had incurred, and announced that he would nullify the documents on settlement of the debt.
