HCM 226
Late 13th. century
1. Nicholas de la Calewe; 5 @. Domini Robert called the young and William
 his néphew, chaplains.
 GRANT in fee-farm, in consideration of eight shillings, of half an acre of arable land in teritorio de Chirchebreynton' lying in the field called ruitehulle between the land of the dean and chapter of Hereford and the land formerly of Roger Lucas and extending as far as Brungewalle siche.
 Rent: one clove annually at the Feast of St. Ethelbert.
 Witnesses: John Lythfot, bailiff of the chapter, Bartholomew called the apothecary (apothecarius), John de la Berne, John le
 ypward, ip Howell', Roger franc’, Latin.
