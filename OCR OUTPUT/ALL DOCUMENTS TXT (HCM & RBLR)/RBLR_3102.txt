803 18 January 1331 London. Commission to M. Robert de Worth, canon of Salisbury, the Official, to Ds. Adam de Herwynton, and Ds. Roger de Breynton, canons of Hereford, rectors respectively of Tredington and Hanbury-by-Droitwich. Because he is about to go aborad on certain arduous business touching the king and his realm, the bishop is unable to administer the spiritualities of his diocese. He appoints them, jointly and severally, to act as his vicars during such absence.
