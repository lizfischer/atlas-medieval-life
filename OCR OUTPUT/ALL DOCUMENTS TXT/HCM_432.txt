HCM 432
1305, Wednesday next after the Feast of St. Edmund Archbishop [November 17]. 
1. John de Aqua Blanea, dean of the church of St. Bthelbert of Hereford, and the chapter of the same; 2. Dowinus William Mortimer, their fellow canon. GRANT in fee-farm for life of the manor of Little Pyon, formerly held by magister William de la Haye. Rent: fifty loads of crops (bladi) annually, namely twenty-five loads of good corn dried and winnowed and twenty-five loads of good oats dried and well winnowed at two terms. Right of re-entry is reserved to the the grantors if the rent remains unpaid for one year. Given the chapter-house at Hereford. Latin. Mutilated.
