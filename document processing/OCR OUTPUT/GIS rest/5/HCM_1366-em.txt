1317, 11 Kal. September [August 22],
LETTER of Adam, bishop of Hereford, addressed to the dean and chapter of Hereford as collectors in the city, deanery, and archdeaconry of Hereford of the tenth for six years lately imposed by Pope Clement V. The bishop quotes a writ he has received m King Edward II dated the first day of August 1317, which says that since the king is in dire need of money to meet urgent affairs, the bishop is to pay into the royal treasury for the remainder of the of the first year of the tenth for six years imposed by Pope Clement V at the Council of Vienne to support the general crusade, and lent to the king by Pope John XXII, The payment is to be made by the morrow of the feast of the nativity of the blessed Virgin Mary next
approaching, The bishop therefore exhorts the dean and chapter of Hereford to apply themselves with all diligence to collection and payment of the said tenth, and to inform him of their action in due time, Given at Thame. Latin. Romnants of seal.
