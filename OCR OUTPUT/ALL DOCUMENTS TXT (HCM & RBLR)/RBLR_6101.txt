No. 1448. 1361, Wednesday next after the Feast of Saint Thomas the Apostle, namely December 22. A. RECEIPT of dominus Lewis, bishop of Hereford, to the chapter of the cathedral church of Hereford for the following items by the hand of magister Griffin de Charleton', canon of Hereford:- a pontifical book bound in red leather, a gradual with a trope bound in leather; a vestment with a stole, maniple, and amice of the gift of John de Brecon'; a vestment with a stole, maniple, and amice of the gift of domina Eleanore, the Queen; a vestment with a stole, maniple, and amice of the gift of Thomas Charleton', formerly bishop [of Hereford]; a chasuble of the gift of Henry Schoorne; a white mantle of the gift of the bishop of Exeter, a white mantle of the gift of Roger de Breynton'; two pieces of Plunket cloth, spotted, with golden leopards (duos pannos de Plonkett' pulverisat' cum leopardis aureis); a mitre which belonged to Richard, formerly bishop of Hereford; a pair of sandals, a pair of shoes; one pair of gloves; one pontifical ring, nine of its stones being missing; one dalmatic of gold cloth; a white mitre of the gift of Grauntson'; another white used mitre for consecration; a pastoral staff of wood with a head of silver and gold; two towels for the pastoral staff; a censer with silver chains. All these are to be returned to the cathedral church. Given at Hereford. Latin. Broken seal.
